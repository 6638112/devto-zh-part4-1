# 如何以正确的方式建立你的投资组合

> 原文：<https://dev.to/hussein_cheayto/how-to-build-your-portfolio-the-right-way-3akj>

"对知识的投资总是会带来最大的收益."本杰明·富兰克林。你的知识和经验是你最重要的职业资产。不幸的是，他们的资产即将到期。随着新技术、语言和环境的发展，你的知识变得过时了。鉴于网络时代的飞快速度，这可能会让你的经历变得过时或无关紧要。随着你的知识价值下降，你对公司或客户的价值也在下降。为了防止这种情况发生，这个快速指南将帮助你建立完美的投资组合。查看我的[作品集](https://husseincheayto.com)网站。我很高兴指导你建立自己的投资组合。

### **A-建立你的投资组合**

管理知识投资组合与管理金融投资组合非常相似:

#### **1。定期投资的习惯。**

就像金融投资一样，你必须定期投资你的知识投资组合。即使只是少量，习惯本身也和总数一样重要。

#### **2。多元化是长期成功的关键。**

你知道的不同的东西越多，你就越有价值。今天的技术明天可能就几乎没用了。你对越多的技术感到舒服，你就越能适应变化。

#### **3。管理风险**

不要把所有的技术鸡蛋放在一个篮子里。将所有资金投资于可能突然崩盘的高风险股票并不是一个好主意，也不应该保守地全部投资，错过可能的机会。

#### **4。低买高卖**

在一项新兴技术变得流行之前学习它就像发现一只被低估的股票一样困难，但是回报是一样的。在 Java 刚出现的时候学习它可能会有风险，但对于那些现在处于该领域顶端的早期采用者来说，这是一笔丰厚的回报。另一个例子是 Kotlin，它可能在不久的将来取代 Java。如果你是程序员，尽快学习 Kotlin。

#### **5。投资组合应定期审查和重新平衡。**

这是一个充满活力的行业。你上个月开始研究的热门技术现在可能已经凉透了。也许你需要温习一下你有一段时间没用过的数据库技术。或者，如果你尝试了其他技术，你可能会在新的职位空缺中处于更有利的位置。要想在职业生涯中获得成功，你必须使用同样的指导方针来管理你的知识组合。

### **B-设定你的目标**

现在你已经有了一些关于什么和什么时候添加到你的知识投资组合中的指导方针，那么获取知识资本来投资你的投资组合的最佳方式是什么呢？这里有几个建议:

#### **阅读阅读阅读**

Read technical and nontechnical books every quarter. Bookstores are full of books on interesting topics related to your current project. Once you’re in the habit, read a book a month.

**相关文章:** [想发财就看这些书](https://dev.to/hussein_cheayto/read-these-books-if-you-want-to-be-rich-4bgo)

#### **上课。**

Look for interesting courses at your local community college or university, or perhaps at the next trade show that comes to town. If you prefer online courses, check out [Udemy](https://udemy.com) if you're programming oriented.

 **#### 加入本地用户组。** 

Don’t just go and listen, but actively participate. Isolation can be deadly to your career. Sign up to [Meetup](https://secure.meetup.com/register/) where you can find like-minded people.

#### **保持更新。**

Subscribe to trade magazines and other journals. Choose some that cover technology different from that of your current project. If you're interested in programming, subscribe to [Codinstyle's](https://codinstyle.pro/subscribe-codinstyle/) newsletter, and you will get 95% coupons on Udemy courses, 30 minutes for a 1-1 online session with me, and much much more.

#### 走出你的舒适区。

Once you feel comfortable with some new language or a bit of technology, move on. Learn another one. The process of learning will expand your thinking, opening you to new possibilities and new ways of doing things.

#### **健身。**

Working out, especially doing cardio, is a great way to get your brain flowing and to keep your mental health in shape. It’s also a great way to think through difficult decisions or process new information.

#### 和比你聪明的人在一起。

"Show me your friends and I show you your future" Dan Peña.

The people we surround ourselves impact us more than we think, so be sure to surround yourself with smart and motivated people.

### **C-寻找学习和成长的机会**

所以你如饥似渴地阅读，你掌握了你所在领域所有最新的突破性进展，有人问你一个问题。你一点儿也不知道答案是什么，并且毫无顾忌地承认。不要就此打住。把它当成寻找答案的个人挑战。

#### 与导师交谈。

Learning from other people is one of the most effective ways to stay educated.

Also, in today's technology-driven world, having a mentor keeps you sharp with your people skills.

Go to coffee with someone once a month and have a topic planned for both of you. You may be surprised at how much you can learn from one person in an hour.

Don’t know how to find a mentor?[Codinstyle](https://codinstyle.pro/mentors/) is a great place to get started if you need a technical mentor.

Just remember: If you don’t ask, the answer is always no.

If you can’t find the answer yourself, find out who can. Don’t let it rest.

All of this reading and researching takes time, and time is already in short supply. So you need to plan ahead. Always have something to read in a dead moment. Time spent waiting for doctors and dentists can be a great opportunity to catch up on your reading—but be sure to bring your own magazine with you.

1- Know exactly what you want to ask, and be as specific as you can be.

2- Frame your question carefully and politely. Remember that you’re asking a favor; don’t seem to be demanding an answer.

3- Once you’ve framed your question, stop and look again for the

4- Decide if you want to ask publicly or privately.
